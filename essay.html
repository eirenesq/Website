As mentioned on the main page, I hold a bachelor's degree from St. Petersburg State University. What wasn't mentioned, is that I obtained my degree specifically from the Liberal Arts and Sciences faculty. I won't go into the details regarding the education itself and the disgraceful fall of this system; here I want to store and make public some of the essays (which was the main assessment method) I'd written during my 4 years at the faculty.


Some of the essays were originally written in English, while the majority of the body of my work was completed in Russian, my native language.

Literary School of Poetry, 2019


In 2019, I won the regional All-Russian Olympiad of schoolchildren in Literature. I was just able to write good essays, and analysing the literature was easy for me, but I wasn't that much invested into this subject. However, after I'd won the Olympiad, I found out that my mother had enrolled me into an education centre for gifted children. I went to Sochi for a whole month of February. At the time, I was desperate to get out. Those of you who are lucky to speak Russian will understand what I mean if they read my literary output in the corresponding section. For those not bestowed with the skill of speaking the mother tongue of Nabokov, I will put it shortly: I was an angsty teen.
